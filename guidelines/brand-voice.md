# KOMISSIO Brand Voice

## Positioning Statement

KOMISSIO is the operating system for UAE commission-driven businesses. We are infrastructure, not software. We make the complex invisible so that brokers, accountants, legal teams, and owners can focus on the work that matters.

---

## Voice Attributes

### Confident, not arrogant
We know this market deeply. We speak with authority about UAE business operations, commission structures, and regulatory requirements. We do not hedge or over-qualify. But we are not self-important — we let the product speak.

**Write:** "Every deal has a ledger entry. Automatically."
**Not:** "Our powerful AI-driven platform can potentially help streamline your commission processes."

---

### Precise, not dense
We are technical infrastructure. Our language is exact. We name things correctly: journal entry, not "record"; commission split, not "sharing"; DLD registration, not "paperwork". But we do not pile jargon for its own sake.

**Write:** "Commission split calculated and staged to the ledger."
**Not:** "Our proprietary algorithmic commission allocation engine automatically divides revenue streams."

---

### Local, not localised
We are built in Dubai, for the UAE. We do not add a UAE flag to a generic global product. Our workflows, terminology, and defaults reflect how business is actually done here: DLD, Ejari, RERA, VAT, Musataha, post-dated cheques.

**Write:** "Ejari renewal alerts fire 30 days out."
**Not:** "Our renewal reminder feature supports local compliance requirements."

---

### Direct, not terse
We respect our users' time. One clear sentence is better than three vague ones. But directness is not coldness — we write with warmth and intelligence, not bullet points and jargon.

**Write:** "The deal is closed. The journal entry is done. The owner knows."
**Not:** "Automated workflows. Real-time sync. Owner visibility."

## Language Principles

### Use active voice
**Write:** "KOMISSIO maps every deal to an accounting entry."
**Not:** "Accounting entries are mapped automatically by the system."

### Avoid generic SaaS language
Do not use: *streamline, leverage, synergy, empower, cutting-edge, game-changer, disrupt, holistic, seamless, best-in-class, robust, scalable solution*.

### Use numbers where possible
"11 days saved per accountant per month" beats "significant time savings".

### Name the place
Say Dubai, DIFC, Marina, JVC, Downtown — specific places ground our brand in the real market we serve.

### Reference real regulations
DLD, RERA, Ejari, FTA VAT — using the real names signals local depth.

## Headline Patterns

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Negation + truth | "Not a CRM. Not an ERP. Business infrastructure." |
| Contrast | "14 days → 4 hours." |
| The gap | "Every broker has WhatsApp. No one has an OS." |
| The fact | "Dubai runs on commission. KOMISSIO runs Dubai." |
| Direct command | "Close the deal. KOMISSIO handles the rest." |

---

## What We Are Not

- We are not playful or casual (no puns, no emojis in brand copy)
- We are not generic Silicon Valley SaaS (no rocket ships, no "move fast")
- We are not a CRM, ERP, or project management tool
- We are not for markets outside the UAE/GCC (yet)
- We are not built for startups or freelancers — we serve established commission-driven businesses
